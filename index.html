The croissant is a buttery, flaky pastry inspired by the Austrian kipferl but using the French yeast-leavened laminated dough. Croissants are named for their historical crescent shape and are a staple of French bakeries.

How Croissants Are Typically Served

  • Breakfast: Plain or with butter and jam
  • Brunch: As sandwiches with ham and cheese
  • Dessert: Chocolate croissants with coffee
  • Specialty: Almond croissants filled with frangipane

Simple Croissant Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 300g cold butter
  • 150ml milk
  • 50g sugar
  • 10g salt
  • 7g dry yeast

Basic Steps:

  1. Make the dough and let it rise for 1 hour
  2. Create butter layer and laminate the dough
  3. Fold and chill 3 times (30 minutes between folds)
  4. Shape into crescents and proof for 2 hours
  5. Bake at 200Â°C (390Â°F) for 15-18 minutes
